MongoInspector for Meteor

#  Less Time in Console, More Time Building
A big part of the Meteor game is publishing and subscribing to data. However, as you change your routes and/or subscriptions, you may accidentally unsubscribe or oversubscribe to data without noticing it. That's why I created this package - to give you a real-time window into how your application and collections are operating. It's great for beginners to observe how collections work, and for experts to build even faster.

#  Main Benefits
• Watch your collections update in real-time in the browser (see bottom left corner)
• Click on a collection to run "console.log(Collection.find().fetch())"
• Click on the title to remove it from the window

#  How To Install
• Run "meteor add msavin:mongoinspector"

#  Questions & Answers
Q: Will this hurt the performance of my application?
A: Absolutely not. MongoInspector is light (<3kb pre-minifaction), and only lives on the client.

Q: Will this run in production?
A: No, the package uses the debugOnly parameter, so it won't even be compiled into production.

Q: What is the future of MongoInspector?
A: Currently, the package is simple and stable. The ultimate goal is to create a visual way for you to browse your client side data and see how your client is operating in real time. I'd love to hear your feedback on this package and where you think it should go as well.
